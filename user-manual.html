1. Platform Overview

Al-Mishkat is your Islamic educational website. It has two parts:

  • The public website — what all visitors see: subject pages, video hubs, PDF libraries, quizzes, and the Five Pillars. No login needed to browse.
  • The Admin panel — your private, password-protected area where you upload and manage content. Everything you upload immediately appears live on the website.

When you upload a video or PDF and assign it to a subject (e.g., Aqidah), it automatically appears in that subject's Video Hub or PDF Library for visitors to find.

4. Logging into the Admin Panel

The Admin panel is protected by a password. Only someone who knows the password can upload or delete content.

How to log in:

Step 1: Click Admin in the top navigation bar (between "5 Pillars" and the search bar).
Step 2: You will see the Al-Mishkat Admin login screen with a password field.
Step 3: Type your admin password in the field. You can click the eye icon 👁 on the right to show or hide the characters as you type.
Step 4: Click "Enter Admin Panel". If the password is correct, you will be taken directly to the Content Management area.
Tip — Stay logged in: Once you log in, you stay logged in for the rest of your browser session. You do not need to re-enter the password every time you visit the Admin page, as long as you keep the browser tab open.

How to lock the Admin panel:

When you are finished uploading and want to secure the panel, click the "Lock" button at the top right of the Content Management page. This immediately returns to the password screen.

The Admin panel also locks automatically when you close your browser or open a fresh browser session.

If you forget your password: The password is stored in the Replit Secrets settings of your project. Contact your developer to reset it.

5. How to Upload a Video (with Thumbnail)

Follow these steps to add a video that will appear in a subject's Video Hub tab.

Step 1 — Log into Admin: Click Admin in the header and enter your password.
Step 2 — "Upload Video" tab: It is the first tab — selected by default when you open the Admin panel.
Step 3 — Select your video file: Click the left upload area labelled "Click to select video". Choose an MP4, MOV, AVI, or WebM file from your computer.
Step 4 — Add a thumbnail image (recommended): Click the right upload area labelled "Click to add thumbnail". Choose a JPG, PNG, or WebP image. This image will appear as the preview card for your video on the subject page. If you skip this, a generic icon is shown instead.
Step 5 — Enter the Title: Type a clear title, e.g. "Introduction to Aqidah — Part 1".
Step 6 — Select the Subject: Click the Subject dropdown and choose where this video belongs (Aqidah, Tafsir, Hadith, Fiqh, Tarbiyah, or Sirah).
Step 7 — Choose the Type:
  • Lecture — Full-length educational talk (typically 20 minutes or longer)
  • Short — A focused clip (3–20 minutes)
  • Reel — A very short social-style clip (under 3 minutes)
Step 8 — Enter Duration (optional): Type the video length, e.g. "45:00" or "1 hour 20 min".
Step 9 — Write a Description (optional): A brief summary of what the video covers. This helps visitors know what to expect.
Step 10 — Click "Upload Video": A progress bar will appear. It uploads the video first, then the thumbnail (if added), then saves the details. A green confirmation appears when done.
After uploading: Go to Subjects → (your subject) → Video Hub to confirm your video card appears with its thumbnail.

6. How to Upload a PDF

Follow these steps to add a PDF that will appear in a subject's PDF Library tab.

Step 1 — Log into Admin: Click Admin in the header and enter your password.
Step 2 — Click "Upload PDF" tab: The second tab in the Admin panel (next to "Upload Video").
Step 3 — Select your PDF file: Click the upload area and select a .PDF file from your computer.
Step 4 — Enter the Title: For example: "Aqidah al-Wasitiyyah — Arabic Text".
Step 5 — Select the Subject: Choose from the dropdown — Aqidah, Tafsir, Hadith, Fiqh, Tarbiyah, or Sirah.
Step 6 — Choose the Category:
  • Book — A complete Islamic book or classical text
  • Article — A scholarly or academic paper
  • Notes — Study notes or summaries
  • Worksheet — Exercises or student worksheets
Step 7 — Enter Page Count (optional): How many pages the document has.
Step 8 — Write a Description (optional): What is this PDF about?
Step 9 — Click "Upload PDF": Wait for the upload bar to complete. A green success message will appear.
After uploading: Go to Subjects → (your subject) → PDF Library to verify your document is listed.

8. Managing the Content Library

The Content Library tab (third tab in the Admin panel) shows everything that has been uploaded to the database so far.

What you can do:

  • View all videos — see every uploaded video with its subject, type, and duration.
  • View all PDFs — see every uploaded PDF with its subject, category, and page count.
  • Delete content — click the red bin icon next to any item to permanently remove it from the database and the website.
Warning: Deleting an item from the Content Library removes it from the website immediately and permanently. There is no undo. Always double-check before deleting.
Tip — Wrong subject? If you uploaded a file to the wrong subject, delete it from the Content Library and re-upload it with the correct subject selected.

10. Using the Search

The search bar is at the top right of every page. Type any keyword — a topic, subject name, scholar name, or title — and press Enter. The search results page will show matching videos, PDFs, and other content from across the platform.

11. Tips & Quick Reference

Best video format: Use MP4 for best compatibility across all devices and browsers.
Best thumbnail size: Use a 16:9 image (e.g. 1280×720 pixels or 1920×1080 pixels) for the best appearance on video cards.
Best PDF format: Always use .PDF files. Scanned documents work fine as long as they are saved as PDF.
Content goes live immediately: As soon as an upload succeeds and you see the green confirmation, the content is live on the website. No separate publishing step needed.
Changing your password: Your admin password is stored in the Replit Secrets panel. Contact your developer to update it, or you can change it yourself in the Secrets tab if you have access to the Replit project.
Locking the panel: Always click the Lock button (top right of the Admin panel) when you are done uploading, especially if others can access your computer.
